Sony WH-1000XM6 Review: Still the Noise-Canceling King

After six weeks of daily commuting and three transatlantic flights, here's whether Sony's latest flagship is worth the upgrade.

By Paul Boom · May 12, 2026

Sony's WH-1000X line has been the default recommendation for premium wireless headphones for the better part of a decade. The XM6 doesn't reinvent the formula — it sharpens it. New drivers, a more refined headband, and noise canceling that genuinely surprised us on a noisy 7-hour flight.

Our Pick

Sony WH-1000XM6

Best overall noise-canceling headphones in 2026. Comfortable for all-day wear, best-in-class ANC, 32-hour battery.

$399 on Amazon →

What's new vs the XM5

The headband is the most noticeable change in the hand — Sony shaved 12 grams off the previous model and the clamping force is gentler. After eight hours of wear during a transatlantic flight, we didn't have the temple soreness that the XM5 sometimes produced.

"The noise canceling on a 737 was good enough that I forgot I was on a plane. That doesn't happen with most headphones."

Audio quality is the area where Sony was already class-leading and the XM6 nudges it forward without changing the signature. Bass is tight, midrange is more open than before, and the treble has lost a hint of the sibilance that some critics flagged on the XM5.

Battery life and quick charge

Sony rates the XM6 at 32 hours with ANC on, and our testing put it at around 29 — close enough. The bigger win is the quick charge: 10 minutes on USB-PD nets you about 5 hours of playback, which we can confirm.
